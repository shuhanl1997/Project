Brainstorm & sketch

The artifact is my second project from IAT100. The theme of this project is to compose a short comic story based on an existing film or written piece. The project also requires building on photo sequence and adjusting the effects on photos making them look like comics. To begin with, I created a mind map to help me determine the horror topic.

Create a storyboard

Subsequently, I started to create my storyboard, adopting desired shot angles, shot types and strategies when shooting my story. However, I found it difficult to tell the story in 10 to 20 frames as required.

Build a storyline

To solve this problem, I built a brief storyline and drew a storyboard to describe the plot. Next, the storyboard was modified according to the storyline keep only key frames and concentrated more on the development of the story. I also employed diverse orders of the key images to ensure the story to be easy for the readers to understand.



Shooting & Editing


When I started editing the photos, another problem was that the project required the use of diverse skills, including the use of software (e.g., photoshops, illustrator, Lightroom and indesign) to create the layout and endow those photos with comic effects. I was not professional with the software. Also, since it was my first year in university, it became much more difficult for me to learn everything in a few weeks. Thus, I spent at least three hours per day learning the software on lynda.com, youtube.com, and finding professors and TA to ask for advice and help me in software training. The experience when building this project has brought me a chance to learn diverse software and knowing how to use time efficiently in learning new things and communicate with professors and TAs.


Final work

During the whole process of making this comic book, I learned skills of using diverse softwares, and with the courage and help of my friends and teachers, I feel it is mandatory that a designer should focus on his/her artwork, work hard to learn new things and keep exploring new fields.
